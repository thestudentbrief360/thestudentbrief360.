What Is a Brief?

A brief is a short, structured response that makes a clear point and explains why it matters.

1

Claim

A clear stance in 1 to 2 sentences.

2

Reasoning

2 to 4 points that explain and support the claim.

3

Real-World Impact

2 to 3 sentences on how the topic affects students or society.

4

Counterarguments

An optional response to the other side of the issue.

This is a sample format. You can replace this with your real briefs later.

Should Internships Be Paid?

Example Brief

Claim Yes, internships should be paid because students contribute real work and deserve fair compensation.
Reasoning Paid internships recognize student effort, reduce financial barriers, and make opportunities more accessible.
Real-World Impact Paid internships help students from different backgrounds gain experience without taking on financial stress. This can lead to a more diverse and prepared workforce.
Counterargument Some argue internships are mainly for learning. However, learning and fair pay can exist at the same time.
