Abortion Should Be Legal In All States

By Avika Tase • May 26, 2026
Restrictions on abortion access raise questions about bodily autonomy, healthcare access, and whether legal limitations reduce abortions or shift them into less safe environments.

Supporters of nationwide abortion access argue that reproductive decision making is a matter of individual autonomy and personal liberty. They contend that governments should not determine medical choices that directly affect someone's body and future.

Restrictions do not eliminate demand. They can change where and how care is accessed.

Human rights organizations have argued that access to reproductive healthcare intersects with broader rights including privacy, equality, health, and bodily autonomy.

The brief also argues that legal restrictions may increase unsafe practices by creating financial and geographic barriers to care. Travel costs, limited provider availability, and legal uncertainty can affect access.

Health and Broader Impacts

According to public health organizations, unsafe abortion practices can carry serious medical risks. The argument presented is that access to regulated medical care reduces those risks and supports safer outcomes.

Beyond physical effects, the brief discusses emotional and social impacts including stress, isolation, and uncertainty surrounding reproductive decisions.

Counterpoint

Opponents of nationwide legalization may argue that abortion policy should remain under state authority and reflect local values and legal priorities.
