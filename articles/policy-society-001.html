Abortion Should Be Legal In All States

By Avika Tase • 05/26/26

Approximately ninety-eight percent of women ranging from ages fifteen to forty-four do not have access to abortion. As of 2025, around 62.7 million women live in states with abortion bans. Ever since Roe v. Wade was overturned, abortion access has largely been controlled by state governments. Over twenty states have enacted strict laws or complete bans. This raises the question: should abortion be legal in all states? This brief argues that abortion should remain legal nationwide because access is a matter of human rights and because legal restrictions can contribute to unsafe practices.

First, abortion should be legal nationwide because individuals should retain autonomy over decisions involving their own bodies. Supporters argue that governments should not restrict personal reproductive decisions.

“Countries have obligations to respect, protect, and fulfill human rights including sexual and reproductive health and autonomy.”

This perspective argues reproductive liberty extends beyond one issue and connects to broader protections involving equality, privacy, safety, and individual rights.

Amnesty International similarly argues that reproductive autonomy is an essential component of human rights and bodily freedom.

“Forcing someone to carry an unwanted pregnancy or seek unsafe abortion is a violation of human rights.”

Supporters also argue that restrictions can increase unsafe practices when individuals lack financial resources or access to medical care. Costs associated with procedures and travel may create barriers.

According to public health organizations, unsafe abortion methods may involve severe health consequences including infection, heavy bleeding, and long term medical complications.

Supporters argue that restricting legal access does not remove demand but instead shifts care away from regulated medical environments.

Beyond physical health, concerns raised include emotional stress, isolation, anxiety, depression, and broader impacts on well being.
